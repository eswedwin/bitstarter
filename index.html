Some power, automatically switched off on your premises, sold back to the Power Companies during a crisis or daily peak demand, has great value. Do you qualify?

HVAC systems are very good candidates for partial switch-off as they consume large amounts of electricity and the temperatures recover fast when switched back to full on. A WAN connection must available on the premises for most of the communication. A backup link will use the cellular or satellite networks. Our engineer will visit your site and do a survey of systems, applicability of our system to your requirement and vice-versa, and installation requirements. A supply contract between your company and us will be set up for an initial 3-year period.

Our engineers are experts in BMS, HVAC and industrial systems and can thus advise you on the optimum connection. Our equipment will be installed, maintained on your premises by our installation technicians, and hooked up to your systems. The payments start immediately (settled daily) when the system is switched on and could be 1 to 2 percent of your electricity bill while it is just on standby. This increases to about 6 percent of the bill if all available electricity is sold, thus when 25% of your maximum demand is shed. The equipment monitors the total and switched load continuously and determines the energy switched off in a transparent way. The maximum switch off period is limited to two consecutive hours in any 12-hour period with a maximum of four shed periods of a half hour each in the same timespan.

Power Companies:

During a crisis (line trip, line overload, generator failure, etc.) the cloud power station comes online instantaneous (less than five seconds) to make up the shortfall and then keep on generating for up to 2 hours. Useful with tidal power or wind-generators to compensate during sudden dips in the supply.

The Power Companies pay a facility fee of less than 4% of the cost of a similar sized new base-load generation plant over the three-year contract plus a steep price for the energy that is comparable to the cost for peaking-Power Stations. The Power Companies recover some of the money by reselling the power to you after the crisis or peak at a lower rate. (All envisaged loads are recoverable loads.)

Future:

Smaller and more economical units to cover smaller clients that use mostly electricity for energy source. Special design for safe, quick and easy, install and maintenance on the customer premises as this is one of the major costs that do not scale well. Large houses use electric car chargers, electric boilers, under floor heating, washing machines, driers, dishwashers, etc. that are all good candidates for the updated units.

This model is different from the smart-grid systems (and other Virtual Power Stations) in that the client immediately receives something substantial back for the inconvenience of waiting or being slightly away from the optimal temperature. With smart-grid systems all the money stays with the Power Company and all the inconvenience stays with the much smaller client; a typical monopoly relation!

The Power Companies will fight this as it is competition for their most expensive power with the greatest profit. This system also removes them from absolute control and restores some options to the user.

FAQ

What is Bitcoin?
Internet currency (an encrypted number) that exsists solely on the net. It is stored in a Bitcoin wallet on your computer (with paper backup of the codes in your drawer) and can be transfered to anyone, anywhere instantaneous via email. There are physical exchanges where it can easily be exchanged to any physical currency. Example: In this senario the Power Company buys Bitcoin with physical currency and pays this out over time for the standby function rent and energy bought. We receive this daily and immediately pass most of it on to our client generators. The clients can then partial pay their electricity accounts with these Bitcoin, completing the circle in a very transparent and market oriented way.
What does HVAC stand for?
Heating Ventilation and Air-Conditioning.
Why is this model so green?
This type of system is grid stabilizing because of its nearly instantaneous response to any request for extra energy. The Power Companies need less generators on standby/idle to continuous supply the demand and less generating plant need to be build and/or only the cleanest type of generator run.
Why must the grid supply exactly match the demand?
The energy generated in the grid (network) must exactly balance the sum of the energy losses and demand as energy cannot be destroyed or created, it just changes from one form to another. If the generated energy is higher, the generators all speed up causing the frequency and voltage of the grid to increase. When it is lower the frequency and voltage decreases as the generators cannot keep up and thus slow down. As all electric motors run at, or nearly at, the same frequency they also speed up or slow down causing the demand to decrease or increase. The runaway condition that ensues is the instability. The Power Companies mitigate this effect by disconnecting some generating capacity or some demand respectively from the grid, both undesired.
What is a Virtual Power Station?
Instead of generating more power on the grid, the same effect on grid stability can be attained by trimming the demand. Thus "negative demand" is exactly the same as "positive supply" to balance the "supply = demand" equation.

These trades start to get traction when the available power pool grows to the order of 200MW to 300MW (one medium-sized base load generator).
